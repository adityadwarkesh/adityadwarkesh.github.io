Publications and Other Works
Who Killed Augustus Bourbaki?
A short story I wrote in 2020 will be getting published in Black Wings VII (by PS Publishing), an anthology of Lovecraftian horror stories put together by S.T. Joshi. The book will be out in summer 2023, and I'll update this page with a link in time.
Wilderness contra Civilization: A phenomenological perspective
My submission for the 2021 Alpine Fellowship essay. I didn't win anything there, but through the efforts of my dear friend John Borstlap, it became published in the Dutch philosophy magazine "Filosofie-Tijdschrift", in this volume.
The Duty of a Philosopher
An essay I wrote for the MSCP (Melbourne School of Continental Philosophy) 2020 essay contest, with the prompt 'What is philosophy today, what is today for philosophy?'. I still think it was a pretty good effort; sadly, the contest ended up getting cancelled and I still don't know what to do with this.
Understanding 'Understanding'
An essay I wrote in 2019 for the Royal Institute of Philosophy essay contest, the topic being 'The Significance of Paradoxes'. It never really got anywhere.
Fundamentality as a Linguistic Paradigm (and Linguistics as a Fundamental Paradigm)
My submission for the 2017 FQXi (Fundamental Questions Institute) essay contest on the topic of "What is Fundamental?". I won a prize, and it was subsequently published here .
